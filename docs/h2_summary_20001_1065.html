Heritability for 20001_1065

Last updated 2017-09-18


Warning: Small effective sample size (\(N_{eff}=1267\)); estimates are likely unstable.

Phenotype: Cancer code, self-reported: thyroid cancer

Estimated SNP heritability: 0.1459 (se=0.218, p=2.518e-01)

This phenotype can be found on the UK Biobank Showcase for code 20001. GWAS results are available for N = 337,159 unrelated individuals of European ancestry. This is a binary phenotype with 317 cases and 336,842 controls.

Note: Heritability for this binary outcome is reported on the liability scale, assuming that the population prevalence matches the prevalence in the UK Biobank analysis set (0.00094). This may be unreliable if the outcome was limited to a subset of individuals, or if the UK Biobank study population is not representative for this phenotype (which is likely in many cases).

Estimated observed-scale heritability: 0.0016 (se=0.00235).


Additional Results

All results are from partitioned heritability analysis of this phenotype using LD score regression (Bulik-Sullivan et al. 2015, github repo) with 74 annotations as described by Gazal et al. 2017 (also on biorxiv). See Technical Details for more information.


Confounding and Model Misspecification

In addition to heritability, LD score regression also estimates a intercept term that indexes of population stratification, other counfounding, and potential misspecification in the partitioned LD score model for the distribution of genetic effects genome-wide.

  • Intercept: 1.006 (se=0.00884, p=2.483e-01)
  • Mean \(\chi^2\): 1.0137
  • \(\lambda_{GC}\): 1.0128
  • Ratio: 0.4402 (se=0.6474)

Intercept values near 1 indicate little or no confounding. Comparing the intercept and the mean \(\chi^2\) statistic provides an rough index for how much of the polygenic signal in the GWAS may be due to confounding rather than genetic effects (assuming the LD score model is well specified).
