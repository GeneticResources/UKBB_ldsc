Heritability for N02

Last updated 2017-09-14


Phenotype: Diagnoses - main ICD10: N02 Recurrent and persistent haematuria

Estimated SNP heritability: 0.0999 (se=0.195, p=3.046e-01)

This phenotype can be found as part of the ICD code listings on the UK Biobank Showcase for code 41202. GWAS results are available for N = 337,199 unrelated individuals of European ancestry. This is a binary phenotype with 337 cases and 336,862 controls.

Note:

Heritability for this binary outcome is reported on the liability scale, assuming that the population prevalence matches the observed prevalence of 0.000999 in the UK Biobank analysis set. This may be unreliable if the outcome was only defined for a subset of individuals, or if the the UK Biobank study population (which skews older, whiter, and higher SES than the general UK population, among other things) is not a representative sample for this phenotype. For comparison, estimated observed-scale heritability is: 0.0011 (se=0.00222). The p-value is unaffected.


Full Results

All results are from partitioned heritability analysis of this phenotype using LD score regression (Bulik-Sullivan et al. 2015, github repo) with 74 annotations as described by Gazal et al. 2017 (also on biorxiv). See Technical Details for more information.


Confounding and Model Misspecification

In addition to heritability, LD score regression also estimates a intercept term that indexes of population stratification, other counfounding, and potential misspecification in the partitioned LD score model for the distribution of genetic effects genome-wide.

  • Intercept: 0.9918 (se=0.00829, p=8.375e-01)
  • Mean \(\chi^2\): 0.9993
  • \(\lambda_{GC}\): 1.0049
  • Ratio: NA (se=NA)

Intercept values near 1 indicate little or no confounding. Comparing the intercept and the mean \(\chi^2\) statistic provides an rough index for how much of the polygenic signal in the GWAS may be due to confounding rather than genetic effects (assuming the LD score model is well specified).
